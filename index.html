George Wood

I am a sociologist and computational social scientist. My research focuses on social inequality and discrimination, with an emphasis on policing, violence, and the impacts of the criminal legal system. I am interested in applications of social network analysis, causal inference, and machine learning in the social sciences.

I am currently Lecturer in Social Statistics at the University of Manchester.

Before joining Manchester, I was Moore-Sloan Faculty Fellow in the Center for Data Science at New York University. Previously, I was a postdoc at Yale and Northwestern under the supervision of Andrew Papachristos. I completed my PhD in Sociology at the University of Oxford in 2017.

Research:

My research examines sociological questions around inequality, the criminal legal system, and violence. For example:

  • How does the criminal legal system create and maintain inequality?
  • How does race and ethnicity shape policing and police-civilian interactions?
  • How has militarization impacted policing?
  • What are the causes and consequences of gun violence?
  • Which policies and interventions work for preventing gun violence?
  • How can community organizations enhance public safety?

I am interested in causal inference and applications of machine learning. Much of my work examines social networks.
